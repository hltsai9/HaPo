Harry Potter & the Chamber of Secrets

A Character Guide to Book 2

Second year brings whispered warnings, writing on the wall, and something ancient stirring beneath the castle. Here are the witches, wizards, and creatures you'll meet in Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets.

Gryffindor

Daring, nerve, and chivalry

Harry Potter

Second-year and reluctant celebrity. Discovers he can speak Parseltongue — and that the whole school suspects he's the Heir of Slytherin.

Ron Weasley

Breaks his wand in the Whomping Willow crash, spends the year backfiring spells — and heroically enters the Chamber with his broken wand taped together.

Hermione Granger

Brews Polyjuice Potion in a girls' bathroom and figures out the monster is a basilisk — before being Petrified mid-library.

Ginny Weasley

Ron's little sister. Finally at Hogwarts, terrifyingly shy around Harry, and secretly pouring her heart into a blank diary she found in her cauldron.

Colin Creevey

An excitable Muggle-born first-year who never stops taking Harry's picture. His camera may have saved his life when the basilisk caught his eye.

Nearly Headless Nick

The Gryffindor ghost. Throws his 500th Deathday Party, still resentful that he was never admitted to the Headless Hunt (his head is only nearly off).

Slytherin

Cunning, ambition, and the blood of the heir

Draco Malfoy

New Slytherin Seeker, thanks to Daddy's Nimbus 2001 donations. Uses the word "Mudblood" and learns not to try it on Hermione.

Lucius Malfoy

Draco's cold, silver-haired father. Slips a cursed diary into Ginny's cauldron and pays for it when Harry tricks him into freeing Dobby.

Tom Marvolo Riddle

A charming Head Boy from 1943 — or rather, a memory trapped in a diary. Beneath the charm: the sixteen-year-old who became Lord Voldemort.

The Basilisk

Slytherin's hidden monster. A giant serpent whose gaze kills instantly — or Petrifies anyone who sees only its reflection. Lives beneath the school.

Ravenclaw

Wit, learning, and wisdom

Gilderoy Lockhart

Golden-haired, grinning, and spectacularly useless new Defense Against the Dark Arts teacher. Author of many books, memory-charmer of many braver wizards.

Moaning Myrtle

A sullen ghost who haunts the second-floor girls' bathroom. Died in that very spot, fifty years ago, after looking into a pair of enormous yellow eyes.

Hufflepuff

Loyalty, patience, and hard work

Justin Finch-Fletchley

A friendly Muggle-born Hufflepuff. Terrified Harry is out to kill him after the Dueling Club, and gets Petrified for his trouble.

Ernie Macmillan

Loudly certain that Harry is the Heir of Slytherin. To his credit, he apologizes directly once it becomes clear he isn't.

Beyond the Common Rooms

Dobby

A house-elf in a dirty pillowcase. Desperately tries to save Harry's life — by breaking his arm, nearly killing his uncle, and sealing the Hogwarts barrier.

Fawkes

Dumbledore's phoenix. Arrives in the Chamber carrying the Sorting Hat, blinds the basilisk, and cries the healing tears that save Harry's life.

Aragog

The colossal acromantula Hagrid raised in a school cupboard. Blind and ancient now, he lives in the Forbidden Forest with his enormous, hungry family.

Arthur Weasley

Ron's father. Works at the Ministry's Misuse of Muggle Artefacts Office — and secretly bewitches a Ford Anglia to fly.

Molly Weasley

Ron's mother. Sends Howlers with the force of a jet engine, knits Christmas jumpers with dogged love, and keeps the Burrow running on sheer will.

Cornelius Fudge

The fidgety, bowler-hatted Minister of Magic. Under pressure to "do something," he hauls Hagrid off to Azkaban without a shred of evidence.
