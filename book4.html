Harry Potter & the Goblet of Fire

A Character Guide to Book 4

Fourth year brings the Quidditch World Cup, the Triwizard Tournament, foreign visitors, a new Defense Against the Dark Arts teacher, and at the end of it all — Lord Voldemort returned to flesh.

Gryffindor

The fourth champion, by accident

Harry Potter

Unwillingly named the fourth Triwizard champion. Faces a Hungarian Horntail, a lake of merpeople, a hedge maze — and Voldemort himself, returned.

Ron Weasley

Maroon, frilly, second-hand dress robes from Auntie Tessie. Falls out with Harry over the Triwizard, falls for Fleur, and asks Hermione last for the Yule Ball.

Hermione Granger

Founds the Society for the Promotion of Elfish Welfare. Goes to the Yule Ball with Viktor Krum and turns every head — including Ron's, finally.

Slytherin

Old loyalties stir again

Draco Malfoy

Mocks Harry as the fourth champion, then gets transfigured into a bouncing white ferret by "Mad-Eye" Moody. Nicknamed for it the rest of the year.

Ravenclaw

A Yule Ball date and a first crush

Cho Chang

Ravenclaw's Seeker. Politely turns Harry down for the Yule Ball — she's already going with someone else: Cedric Diggory.

Hufflepuff

A champion, a friend, a tragedy

Cedric Diggory

Tall, handsome, fair-minded — chosen by the Goblet as the true Hogwarts champion. Reaches the Triwizard Cup with Harry, then Voldemort orders Wormtail to "kill the spare."

From Beauxbatons & Durmstrang

Viktor Krum

Bulgarian Seeker, Triwizard champion from Durmstrang. Surly on the ground, dazzling in the air. Surprises everyone by asking Hermione to the Yule Ball.

Fleur Delacour

Beauxbatons champion, part-Veela, makes most boys forget how to speak. Calls everything "'Ogwarts" and saves Harry's place in the second task.

Madame Maxime

Headmistress of Beauxbatons. Towering, elegant, and (according to her) "big-boned." Charms Hagrid utterly.

Igor Karkaroff

Headmaster of Durmstrang. Greasy goatee, fur cloak, ex-Death Eater. Flees the night the Dark Mark burns on his arm.

Beyond the Houses

"Mad-Eye" Moody

Or rather, Barty Crouch Jr in his skin. The retired Auror with the magical electric-blue eye is impersonated all year — until the very last chapter.

Rita Skeeter

Daily Prophet's most poisonous reporter. Jewelled spectacles, blood-red nails, and a Quick-Quotes Quill that writes whatever lie sells papers.

Bartemius Crouch Sr

Head of the Department of International Magical Cooperation, fluent in two hundred languages, devoted to the rules. Hides his Death Eater son under the Cloak.

Barty Crouch Jr

Loyal Death Eater. Drinks Polyjuice Potion all year to wear Mad-Eye Moody's face — and arranges for Harry to deliver Voldemort his blood.

Ludo Bagman

Head of Magical Games and Sports, ex-Beater for the Wimbourne Wasps. Booming and boyish, owes goblins a fortune, tries to help Harry in every task.

Winky

The Crouch family house-elf, found with Harry's wand at the Quidditch World Cup. Sacked, drowning her grief in butterbeer and hating Dobby for liking it.

Lord Voldemort

Reborn from his father's bone, Wormtail's flesh, and Harry's blood. The Dark Lord rises again — and only Harry escapes the graveyard alive.

Wormtail (Peter Pettigrew)

Crawls back to Voldemort. Cuts off his own hand to brew the resurrection potion — and is gifted a silver hand in return.

Hagrid

Showed Harry the dragons before the first task and falls heavily for Madame Maxime. His class meets Blast-Ended Skrewts; nobody has fond memories of them.
