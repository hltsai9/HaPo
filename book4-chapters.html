Book 4: Chapter Illustrations

Harry Potter & the Goblet of Fire — a scene from each chapter

Thirty-seven scenes — one per chapter of Goblet of Fire.

Chapter 1: The Riddle House

Frank Bryce, the elderly gardener, sees a light in the abandoned mansion. He climbs the stairs and overhears Voldemort plotting Harry's death.

Chapter 2: The Scar

Harry wakes at Privet Drive with his scar searing — and writes to Sirius for the first time about it.

Chapter 3: The Invitation

An over-stamped letter from Mrs. Weasley arrives — inviting Harry to the Quidditch World Cup.

Chapter 4: Back to The Burrow

The Weasleys come for Harry — and accidentally blow apart the Dursleys' boarded-up fireplace. He's home.

Chapter 5: Weasleys' Wizard Wheezes

Fred and George "accidentally" drop a Ton-Tongue Toffee. Dudley eats it; his tongue grows four feet long.

Chapter 6: The Portkey

On Stoatshead Hill at dawn, the Weasleys, Cedric, and his father Amos all crowd round one moldy old boot.

Chapter 7: Bagman and Crouch

The Weasleys arrive at the World Cup campsite and meet Ludo Bagman in his Wasps robes and the buttoned-up Mr. Crouch.

Chapter 8: The Quidditch World Cup

Ireland's chasers run riot. Krum catches the Snitch for Bulgaria, but Ireland still wins, 170 to 160.

Chapter 9: The Dark Mark

The Death Eater symbol — green skull with serpent tongue — blazes above the campsite. Wizards have not seen it openly cast for thirteen years.

Chapter 10: Mayhem at the Ministry

Rita Skeeter's slanted reporting on the World Cup riot fills the morning Prophet. The Ministry is dragging its feet.

Chapter 11: Aboard the Hogwarts Express

The countryside rolls past. Hermione, Ron, and Harry trade gossip; Malfoy hints something exciting is coming to Hogwarts this year.

Chapter 12: The Triwizard Tournament

Dumbledore announces the resurrected Tournament. Three schools, three champions, eternal glory — and a 1,000-galleon prize.

Chapter 13: Mad-Eye Moody

The new DADA teacher arrives — battle-scarred, clunking on a wooden leg, with a brilliant blue magical eye that swivels on its own.

Chapter 14: The Unforgivable Curses

Moody demonstrates Imperius, Cruciatus, and Avada Kedavra on a spider — to teach students what they may one day face.

Chapter 15: Beauxbatons and Durmstrang

A blue carriage drawn by giant winged horses descends from the sky. From the lake, the Durmstrang ship rises with mast and lantern.

Chapter 16: The Goblet of Fire

The Goblet flares blue. Champions place their names; a slip of parchment flutters out — and a fourth comes after.

Chapter 17: The Four Champions

Cedric Diggory, Fleur Delacour, Viktor Krum — and impossibly, a fourth: Harry Potter, age fourteen, the youngest competitor in centuries.

Chapter 18: The Weighing of the Wands

Mr. Ollivander himself examines each champion's wand. Rita Skeeter and her Quick-Quotes Quill stalk Harry into a cleaning cupboard.

Chapter 19: The Hungarian Horntail

Hagrid leads Harry through the forest at midnight to see what's coming for the First Task: four nesting dragons.

Chapter 20: The First Task

"Accio Firebolt!" Harry outflies the Horntail in arcs and dives to snatch the golden egg.

Chapter 21: The House-Elf Liberation Front

Hermione discovers the kitchens, finds Dobby in pay (and Winky in disgrace), and launches S.P.E.W. — to general bafflement.

Chapter 22: The Unexpected Task

McGonagall announces the Yule Ball. Champions must open the dance — and Harry has no idea how to ask anyone.

Chapter 23: The Yule Ball

The Great Hall transforms into a winter wonderland. Hermione descends the staircase on Krum's arm, and Ron sees her properly for the first time.

Chapter 24: Rita Skeeter's Scoop

Rita's article makes Harry sound like a tearful soap-opera hero. Worse, it accuses Hermione of being a fickle heartbreaker.

Chapter 25: The Egg and the Eye

In the prefects' bathroom Harry hears the egg's underwater song. Then his Map shows Crouch in Snape's office at midnight.

Chapter 26: The Second Task

Gills sprouting on his neck, Harry races to the bottom of the lake to free Ron — and stays to free Fleur's sister too.

Chapter 27: Padfoot Returns

In a cave on a hillside outside Hogsmeade, Harry, Ron, and Hermione meet Sirius — gaunt, hungry, and reading old newspapers.

Chapter 28: The Madness of Mr. Crouch

Bartemius Crouch staggers out of the Forbidden Forest, talking to invisible secretaries — broken, ranting, begging to see Dumbledore.

Chapter 29: The Dream

Harry dozes in Divination and dreams of Voldemort and Wormtail in a high-backed chair, planning a punishment. His scar burns.

Chapter 30: The Pensieve

In Dumbledore's office, Harry falls into a stone basin of silvery memories — and watches the trial of Barty Crouch Jr.

Chapter 31: The Third Task

The Quidditch pitch becomes a maze of twenty-foot hedges. The Triwizard Cup glows green at its centre.

Chapter 32: Flesh, Blood, and Bone

In the Riddles' graveyard, Wormtail brews the resurrection potion. Bone, flesh, blood — and Voldemort rises from the cauldron.

Chapter 33: The Death Eaters

Voldemort summons the Mark. Hooded figures Apparate around him — a silver-masked circle, kneeling at their master's feet.

Chapter 34: Priori Incantatem

Twin-cored wands lock — a golden cage rises around them. Voldemort's victims stream from his wand: Cedric, Bertha, Frank, Lily, James.

Chapter 35: Veritaserum

Three drops of Veritaserum and the false Moody — Barty Crouch Jr — confesses everything to Dumbledore.

Chapter 36: The Parting of the Ways

Dumbledore tells Fudge plainly: Voldemort has returned. Fudge refuses to believe it — and refuses to shake the offered hand.

Chapter 37: The Beginning

The students board the Hogwarts Express to leave for the summer. Out beyond the lake, war is just beginning — but for now, the dawn is gold.
