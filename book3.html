Third year at Hogwarts: a mass murderer has escaped Azkaban, soul-sucking guards circle the castle, and old friends turn out to be something else entirely. Here are the witches, wizards, and creatures of Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban.

Gryffindor

Daring, nerve, and the Marauders' legacy

Harry Potter

Turns 13, runs away on the Knight Bus, and learns that his godfather allegedly betrayed his parents. Masters the Patronus Charm — a silver stag named Prongs.

Ron Weasley

Loses Scabbers, gains a broken leg, and discovers his pet rat of twelve years was a grown man all along. Declares Crookshanks a murderer all year.

Hermione Granger

Secretly carries a Time-Turner so she can take every class at once. Slaps Malfoy, drops Divination, and saves two innocent lives in a single night.

Sirius Black

Harry's godfather. Gaunt, wild-eyed, and framed for murders he never committed. An unregistered Animagus who can turn into a huge black dog called Padfoot.

Remus Lupin

The new Defense Against the Dark Arts teacher — kind, shabby, and secretly a werewolf. Teaches Harry to cast a Patronus. Once called Moony.

Peter Pettigrew

Alias: Scabbers, Ron's aging, toe-missing pet rat. Actually Wormtail — an Animagus who faked his own death and betrayed Harry's parents to Voldemort.

Slytherin

Cunning, ambition, and grudges that never heal

Draco Malfoy

Gets his arm slashed by Buckbeak for calling him "ruddy chicken," then milks the injury for a full term. Dedicates the year to getting Hagrid fired and Buckbeak executed.

Professor Snape

Loathes Lupin from boyhood and loathes Sirius Black even more. Substitutes for a werewolf, tries to hand Sirius to Dementors, and loses his temper spectacularly.

Ravenclaw

Wit, learning, and a new Seeker on the pitch

Cho Chang

Ravenclaw's new Seeker. Harry catches his first glimpse of her on the Quidditch pitch — and promptly forgets to look for the Snitch.

Hufflepuff

Loyalty, patience, and a captain on the rise

Cedric Diggory

Hufflepuff's new Quidditch captain and Seeker. Handsome, honorable, and so fair-minded he tries to replay the match where Harry fainted off his broom.

Beyond the Common Rooms

Professor Trelawney

Bejewelled Divination teacher wreathed in shawls and incense. Predicts Harry's death weekly — but just once a year, utters a real prophecy in an unrecognisable voice.

Buckbeak

A proud grey hippogriff. Let Harry bow before him, bit Draco for not doing the same, and narrowly escaped execution on a rooftop courtesy of a Time-Turner.

The Dementors

The guards of Azkaban. They drain every happy thought — and if they catch you, they'll suck out your soul with the Dementor's Kiss. Dumbledore posts them around the school.

Crookshanks

Hermione's enormous, squashed-faced ginger cat. Part Kneazle, entirely clever — he sees straight through Scabbers and teams up with a stray black dog.

Stan Shunpike

The spotty, self-important conductor of the Knight Bus. Helpful, if you can get a word in sideways, and prone to announcing everything twice.

Cornelius Fudge

Still Minister of Magic, still nervous, still in that lime-green bowler. Personally meets Harry at the Leaky Cauldron and misses the fugitive hiding in plain sight.
