Book 7: Chapter Illustrations

Harry Potter & the Deathly Hallows — a scene from each chapter

Thirty-seven scenes — one per chapter of Deathly Hallows, plus the epilogue.

Chapter 1: The Dark Lord Ascending

At Malfoy Manor's long table, the Death Eaters watch Charity Burbage rotate above them, then die. Nagini eats.

Chapter 2: In Memoriam

Harry reads Rita Skeeter's obituary — full of dark hints — and Elphias Doge's tearful tribute, side by side.

Chapter 3: The Dursleys Departing

For the first time, Dudley speaks kindly to Harry. The Order takes the Dursleys into hiding — they're never seen again.

Chapter 4: The Seven Potters

Six volunteers drink Polyjuice with Harry's hair. Seven Potters fly in seven directions, scattering thirty Death Eaters.

Chapter 5: Fallen Warrior

The Order regroups at the Burrow. Mad-Eye is dead. Hedwig too. George is missing an ear. They are all that's left.

Chapter 6: The Ghoul in Pyjamas

The Weasley family ghoul is dressed in Ron's pyjamas and given spattergroit, a decoy in case anyone comes searching.

Chapter 7: The Will of Albus Dumbledore

Scrimgeour delivers the bequests in person. Three small objects, each a clue Dumbledore left.

Chapter 8: The Wedding

Bill and Fleur marry under a canopy of gold. Then Kingsley's silver lynx lands among the dancers — "The Ministry has fallen."

Chapter 9: A Place to Hide

The trio Disapparate to 12 Grimmauld Place. Dust, decay, and Sirius's old room — and the locket they need is gone.

Chapter 10: Kreacher's Tale

Kreacher tells of Regulus Black — R.A.B., the Slytherin who forced Voldemort to make him drink the cave potion, and stole the locket back.

Chapter 11: The Bribe

Kreacher drags Mundungus back to Grimmauld Place. He confesses: he sold the locket to a Ministry woman — Dolores Umbridge.

Chapter 12: Magic is Might

The Ministry Atrium has a new statue: a witch and wizard sit on a throne built of crushed Muggle bodies, labelled "Magic is Might."

Chapter 13: The Muggle-Born Registration Commission

The trio infiltrate the Ministry to take the locket — and find Umbridge sentencing Muggle-borns to Azkaban.

Chapter 14: The Thief

The trio escape the Ministry into a forest and pitch the tent. The locket lies between them, pulsing with malice.

Chapter 15: The Goblin's Revenge

Eavesdropping on goblins by a riverbank, the trio learn the Sword of Gryffindor in Snape's office is a fake — the real one is gone.

Chapter 16: Godric's Hollow

On Christmas Eve, Harry visits his parents' graves for the first time. \"The last enemy that shall be destroyed is death.\"

Chapter 17: Bathilda's Secret

The old historian is dead. Nagini wears her body like a glove — and bursts loose to attack Harry in Bathilda's bedroom.

Chapter 18: The Life and Lies of Albus Dumbledore

Hermione reads aloud Rita Skeeter's biography. Young Dumbledore befriended Grindelwald. Was the great wizard not who they thought?

Chapter 19: The Silver Doe

A silver doe leads Harry through the snowy wood to a frozen forest pool. Beneath the ice: the true Sword of Gryffindor.

Chapter 20: Xenophilius Lovegood

Atop a hill they find the Lovegoods' rook-shaped house. Xenophilius wears the symbol of the Hallows — and tells them the legend.

Chapter 21: The Tale of the Three Brothers

Death gives them the Wand, the Stone, the Cloak. Two brothers fall to their gifts; the third hides until ready to greet Death as a friend.

Chapter 22: The Deathly Hallows

Triangle, circle, line. Three objects that, mastered together, would conquer Death itself. Harry begins to suspect what Dumbledore left him.

Chapter 23: Malfoy Manor

Captured. Hermione tortured by Bellatrix on the drawing-room floor. Dobby Apparates in, drops the chandelier — and Disapparates with them all.

Chapter 24: The Wandmaker

Ollivander, broken from imprisonment at Malfoy Manor, tells Harry about the Elder Wand and explains the laws of wandlore.

Chapter 25: Shell Cottage

Bill and Fleur's cottage by the sea. Harry digs Dobby's grave by hand — \"Here lies Dobby, a free elf.\"

Chapter 26: Gringotts

Polyjuice Bellatrix, multiplied treasure, the cup found, betrayal, and a desperate ride out on a half-blind dragon.

Chapter 27: The Final Hiding Place

Where would Voldemort hide a piece of his soul? Inside Hogwarts itself — the place where he was first happy.

Chapter 28: The Missing Mirror

From the broken shard of Sirius's two-way mirror, a familiar bright blue eye answers Harry — and sends Aberforth to fetch them.

Chapter 29: The Lost Diadem

The Room of Requirement reveals itself as the Room of Hidden Things: centuries of contraband, and a tarnished tiara on a bust.

Chapter 30: The Sacking of Severus Snape

McGonagall storms the Great Hall and chases Snape from Hogwarts in a swirl of black robes — out a window, like a giant bat.

Chapter 31: The Battle of Hogwarts

The shield over the castle holds against the Death Eaters' first wave. Statues march to war. Students duel for their lives.

Chapter 32: The Elder Wand

In the Shrieking Shack, Voldemort believes Snape is the Elder Wand's master. Nagini ends Snape — but the wand still does not answer.

Chapter 33: The Prince's Tale

Snape's memories pour out of him as he dies. From childhood with Lily to the night Dumbledore asked Snape to kill him — for the greater good.

Chapter 34: The Forest Again

Harry walks willingly into the Forbidden Forest, the Resurrection Stone in his palm. His mother, his father, Sirius, Lupin walk with him.

Chapter 35: King's Cross

A bright, white version of King's Cross. Dumbledore is waiting — and a wretched, wailing thing under a bench. The choice is Harry's.

Chapter 36: The Flaw in the Plan

Dawn in the Great Hall. Expelliarmus meets Avada Kedavra. The Elder Wand flies to its true master. Voldemort falls, and stays fallen.

Epilogue: Nineteen Years Later

King's Cross. Harry sees his children off to Hogwarts. Albus Severus, named for two headmasters of Hogwarts. The scar has not pained Harry for nineteen years.
