Book 2 — Reviews

Harry Potter & the Chamber of Secrets — Full-Cast Audio Edition (Audible, December 2025)

The second instalment in the full-cast series. Reviewers focus on how the production handles a darker, more claustrophobic book: the haunted bathroom, the petrifications, the long descent into the Chamber itself, and the disembodied voice of a teenage Tom Riddle.

The bathroom scenes & the Chamber

Production highlight

The two big set-pieces reviewers return to are the acoustically wet, tiled bathroom where Myrtle haunts — a real test of the Dolby Atmos spatial mix — and the slow, low-frequency hum of the Chamber itself, where the score recedes almost to nothing so that the listener can hear the basilisk's slither in the walls.

The diary's voice

Production design choice

Tom Riddle's voice from the enchanted diary is treated with a processed close-mic effect that reviewers describe as both intimate and unnerving — as if the listener, like Harry, is being whispered to directly.

The Dobby question

Performance debate

Dobby is the breakout casting question of Book 2. Reviewers split: some hear a Dobby that finally has the tremor and oddness the page calls for; others find the voicing too close to the Toby Jones film performance and want something more distinct on audio. Tom Riddle — the teenage voice

Cast highlight

The teenage Riddle is treated as a separate casting from Macfadyen's adult Voldemort — deliberately so. The performance reviewers describe is eerily controlled: polite, articulate, plausibly the favourite student of every professor he met. The horror works because the voice doesn't telegraph the villain.

Versus Dale & Fry on Chamber of Secrets specifically

Cross-edition take

The two single-narrator editions each have famous Chamber-specific moments: Jim Dale's Dobby is iconic to a generation of US listeners, and Fry's quiet handling of the Riddle/diary scenes is often cited as the benchmark. The full-cast edition replaces those virtuoso solo turns with something more layered — whether that improves on or just differs from the originals is the comparison question reviewers keep returning to.
