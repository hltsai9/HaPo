Book 5: Chapter Illustrations

Harry Potter & the Order of the Phoenix — a scene from each chapter

Thirty-eight scenes — one per chapter of Order of the Phoenix.

Chapter 1: Dudley Demented

Two Dementors attack Harry and Dudley in the Privet Drive underpass. Harry casts his stag Patronus and saves them both.

Chapter 2: A Peck of Owls

Owls flood through Privet Drive's window: expulsion notices, retractions, and a desperate "DON'T DO ANYTHING" from Mr. Weasley.

Chapter 3: The Advance Guard

Tonks, Lupin, Mad-Eye, Kingsley and the rest of the Advance Guard arrive on broomsticks to fly Harry to Order HQ.

Chapter 4: Number Twelve, Grimmauld Place

The hidden house of Black materialises between numbers 11 and 13 — peeling, gloomy, full of dark family heirlooms.

Chapter 5: The Order of the Phoenix

Around the long kitchen table at Grimmauld Place: Lupin, Sirius, Tonks, Kingsley, Moody — Dumbledore's resurrected secret society.

Chapter 6: The Noble and Most Ancient House of Black

Cleaning out the drawing room: Sirius shows Harry the family tapestry, his own face burned out by his mother.

Chapter 7: The Ministry of Magic

Mr. Weasley walks Harry through the cavernous Atrium with its golden Fountain of Magical Brethren.

Chapter 8: The Hearing

Harry faces the full Wizengamot in Courtroom Ten. Dumbledore arrives just in time — and Harry walks free.

Chapter 9: The Woes of Mrs. Weasley

Molly faces a boggart that takes the shape of each of her family — dead. Lupin sends it back into the wardrobe.

Chapter 10: Luna Lovegood

On the train, the trio meet a girl reading The Quibbler upside down: Luna, who can see the thestrals too.

Chapter 11: The Sorting Hat's New Song

The Hat sings a warning this year: stand together, lest the founders' divisions destroy us all.

Chapter 12: Professor Umbridge

"Hem hem." Pink cardigan, kitten brooch, and a textbook with no spells in it. Welcome to Defense Against the Dark Arts.

Chapter 13: Detention with Dolores

Umbridge's blood-quill carves "I must not tell lies" into the back of Harry's hand — over and over, all evening.

Chapter 14: Percy and Padfoot

A pompous letter from Percy disowns Ron. Sirius sneaks back as Padfoot to share Christmas in the snow.

Chapter 15: The Hogwarts High Inquisitor

The Ministry promotes Umbridge to inspect — and grade — every other professor at Hogwarts.

Chapter 16: In the Hog's Head

Twenty-eight students cram into the dingy pub for a secret meeting. Hermione proposes — they need to teach themselves DADA.

Chapter 17: Educational Decree Number Twenty-Four

By morning, every student club is forbidden — including Quidditch teams. Conveniently timed for the would-be DA.

Chapter 18: Dumbledore's Army

Behind a wall on the seventh floor: a room of dummies, books, and sneakoscopes. The DA has its first real lesson.

Chapter 19: The Lion and the Serpent

Ron's nervous Keeper debut. Slytherins jeer "Weasley is our King" — until Gryffindor wins, and the song flips.

Chapter 20: Hagrid's Tale

Hagrid, battered and late, tells Harry the story of his journey to the giants — and what he brought back.

Chapter 21: The Eye of the Snake

Harry dreams as a great serpent attacks Mr. Weasley. The dream is real — Nagini, in the Department of Mysteries.

Chapter 22: St. Mungo's Hospital for Magical Maladies and Injuries

Through the dummy in the shop window: a hospital of Magical Bites, Plant Poisonings, Spell Damage — and a wounded Mr. Weasley.

Chapter 23: Christmas on the Closed Ward

The trio meet Neville's parents — locked away on the long-term ward, broken by Bellatrix Lestrange's curses long ago.

Chapter 24: Occlumency

Snape attempts to teach Harry to seal his mind — by ripping into it. The lessons go badly. Quickly.

Chapter 25: The Beetle at Bay

Hermione catches Rita Skeeter — an unregistered beetle Animagus — in a sealed glass jar. Now Rita owes her a favour.

Chapter 26: Seen and Unforeseen

Trelawney sacked publicly, Firenze installed in her place. Harry's nightmares grow more vivid — and more dangerous.

Chapter 27: The Centaur and the Sneak

Marietta betrays the DA — and breaks out in cursed pimples spelling SNEAK across her face. Firenze takes Trelawney's class.

Chapter 28: Snape's Worst Memory

James and Sirius hang fifteen-year-old Snape upside down by the lake. Lily defends him — and he calls her a Mudblood.

Chapter 29: Career Advice

McGonagall declares she will help Harry become an Auror "if it is the last thing I do." Umbridge tries — and fails — to stop her.

Chapter 30: Grawp

Hagrid takes Harry and Hermione to meet his sixteen-foot half-brother, tied to a tree in the Forbidden Forest.

Chapter 31: O.W.L.s

Ordinary Wizarding Levels. A week of theory and practical exams that decide which careers a fifteen-year-old can pursue.

Chapter 32: Out of the Fire

Harry uses Umbridge's office fireplace to call Grimmauld Place — and Sirius is safe at home, just as Voldemort planned.

Chapter 33: Fight and Flight

Hermione lures Umbridge into the Forbidden Forest; the centaurs carry her off. Harry, Ron, Hermione, Luna, Neville and Ginny fly to London on thestrals.

Chapter 34: The Department of Mysteries

Rows on rows of glowing prophecy orbs in the Hall of Prophecy. One has Harry's name on it.

Chapter 35: Beyond the Veil

In the Death Chamber, Bellatrix's curse hits Sirius — and he falls backwards through the veiled archway, gone.

Chapter 36: The Only One He Ever Feared

Dumbledore versus Voldemort in the Atrium. Spells reshape the air; the Ministry sees the truth at last.

Chapter 37: The Lost Prophecy

In Dumbledore's office: Trelawney's first real prediction, sixteen years ago, about the boy who could destroy the Dark Lord.

Chapter 38: The Second War Begins

Front-page headlines tell the truth at last. The wizarding world braces for war.
