Harry Potter & the Philosopher's Stone

A Character Guide to Book 1

Characters Take the Quiz

Welcome, first-year. Below you'll find the wizards, witches, and magical folk you'll meet in Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone — sorted by the Hogwarts house they call home.

Gryffindor

Daring, nerve, and chivalry — the lion's den

Harry Potter

The Boy Who Lived. Lightning-bolt scar, black hair, round glasses. Orphaned son of James and Lily, raised by the Dursleys.

Ron Weasley

Harry's first friend on the Hogwarts Express. Flame-red hair, freckles, and a hand-me-down rat named Scabbers.

Hermione Granger

Brilliant, bossy, and brave. Muggle-born with bushy brown hair. The best student in her year — and never afraid to prove it.

Neville Longbottom

Forgetful, nervous, and kinder than he knows. Raised by his gran, with a stubborn toad named Trevor who keeps escaping.

Professor McGonagall

Head of Gryffindor and Transfiguration teacher. A stern witch who can turn into a tabby cat — square markings around the eyes.

Fred & George Weasley

The mischief-making Weasley twins. Beaters on the Gryffindor Quidditch team, gleefully plotting jokes and breaking rules.

Percy Weasley

Ron's pompous older brother and a Gryffindor prefect. Loves rules, reminding people of the rules, and his shiny prefect badge.

Slytherin

Cunning, ambition, resourcefulness — the serpent's den

Draco Malfoy

Pale-haired, pale-faced, and never short of an insult. Son of Lucius Malfoy. Harry refused his handshake on the first day.

Vincent Crabbe

Heavy-set and slow-witted. One of Draco's two bodyguards. Rarely speaks, often glowers.

Gregory Goyle

Draco's second bodyguard. Just as large as Crabbe and just as unlikely to solve a riddle. Always scowling.

Professor Snape

Head of Slytherin and Potions master. Hooked nose, sallow skin, greasy black hair. Loathes Harry from the moment they meet.

Marcus Flint

Slytherin Quidditch captain. Big, burly, and famously troll-like. Does whatever it takes to win a match.

Ravenclaw

Wit, learning, and wisdom — the eagle's perch

Professor Flitwick

Tiny, squeaky-voiced Charms master and Head of Ravenclaw. Stands on a stack of books to see over his desk.

Penelope Clearwater

A Ravenclaw prefect briefly glimpsed in the halls — clever, composed, and secretly admired by Percy Weasley.

The House of Eagles

Book 1 focuses on the other houses, but Ravenclaws win honors quietly — and the eagle on their crest is always watching.

Hufflepuff

Loyalty, patience, and hard work — the badger's sett

Professor Sprout

Head of Hufflepuff and Herbology teacher. Short, plump, and always a bit covered in earth from the greenhouses.

Ernie Macmillan

A stout, honorable Hufflepuff first-year. Takes pride in his pure-blood heritage and in being thoroughly fair.

The House of Badgers

Helga Hufflepuff said she would take "the lot" — Hufflepuffs are loyal friends, fair minds, and steady hands.

Beyond the Common Rooms

Albus Dumbledore

Headmaster of Hogwarts. Long silver hair, half-moon spectacles, a passion for sherbet lemons, and more magic than almost anyone alive.

Rubeus Hagrid

Keeper of Keys and Grounds. Half-giant, fully hairy, and endlessly fond of dangerous creatures. Flies a motorbike. Loves Harry like family.

Professor Quirrell

The stuttering Defense Against the Dark Arts teacher. Wears a large purple turban that smells strongly of garlic.

Argus Filch

The bitter Hogwarts caretaker. Always prowling the corridors with his scrawny cat Mrs. Norris, hoping to catch a student breaking rules.

Madam Hooch

The flying instructor. Short gray hair, yellow hawk-like eyes. She gives Neville a broken wrist and Harry his spot on the team.

Lord Voldemort

The Dark Wizard whose name most witches fear to speak. Defeated eleven years ago by the baby Harry — but not, as we learn, truly gone.
